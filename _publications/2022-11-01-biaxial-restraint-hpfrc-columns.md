This work addressed the combined effects of biaxial bending and axial restraint on hybrid fiber reinforced high-performance concrete columns at elevated temperatures. It is significant because these coupled restraint and bending conditions are closer to real fire-exposed structural frames than isolated uniaxial column tests.
