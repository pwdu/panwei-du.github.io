My research combines mechanics, experiments, numerical simulation, and machine learning to understand and predict the behaviour of structures and materials under extreme environments.

AI4Science for Structural Engineering
======

I am interested in neural operators, transfer learning, and physics-informed machine learning for fast, reusable simulation models. A current focus is thermal analysis in concrete structures, where neural operators can learn mappings from initial and boundary conditions to temperature fields and support rapid evaluation across fire scenarios.

Structural Fire Engineering
======

My work studies the fire response of reinforced concrete, high-strength concrete, ultra-high-performance concrete, fibre-reinforced concrete, and composite columns. This includes heat transfer, material degradation, explosive spalling, load eccentricity, restraint effects, fire endurance, and practical analytical models for design.

Protective Engineering
======

I work on structural performance under severe loads including blast, impact, and sequential hazards. This direction connects high-temperature damage, residual capacity, and dynamic response of concrete and composite members.

Sustainable and Advanced Cementitious Materials
======

I contribute to studies on fibre-reinforced lightweight aggregate concrete, UHPC, cementitious passive cooling materials, and concrete systems designed for resilience, durability, and lower-carbon performance.

Methods and Tools
======

* Neural operators, transfer learning, and surrogate modelling
* Finite-element modelling and heat-transfer analysis
* Experimental mechanics at ambient and elevated temperatures
* Analytical modelling for columns, slabs, beams, and bond behaviour
* Data processing and scientific Python workflows
